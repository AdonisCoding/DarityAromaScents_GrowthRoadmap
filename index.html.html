Executive Overview

The business challenge — and
why sequence matters.

Darity Aroma Scents has something most early-stage brands struggle to clearly own — a sensory product with genuine gifting appeal, lifestyle positioning, and real multi-channel potential across Shopify, Etsy, and eventually Amazon.

The challenge is not the product. The challenge is sequencing the growth work correctly.

Too many early-stage eCommerce brands rush toward paid advertising before their store is genuinely worth arriving at. They send cold traffic to product pages that feel unfinished. They run ads before their tracking is reliable enough to tell them what's working. They invest in acquisition before they've built any retention economics — which means every customer they earn is also a customer they're likely to lose.

Why this plan is phased this way

1

Foundation first. Before traffic is sent anywhere, the store needs to be worth arriving at. Product pages, trust architecture, tracking, and offer structure come before acquisition.

2

Systems before scaling. Retention, email flows, content engines, and reviews compound quietly — but only if they're built before you're paying to acquire every customer twice.

3

Acquisition on a proven foundation. Paid social, Etsy expansion, and a creative testing engine only perform reliably when the infrastructure underneath them is solid enough to hold the weight.

Three-Phase Structure at a Glance

Phase 1 — Foundation

Store, copy, tracking, bundles, initial creative tools

Phase 2 — Systems

Email flows, content engine, reviews, automation, lead magnets

Phase 3 — Growth

Paid acquisition, Etsy, creative testing, loyalty, Q2+ expansion

2
Systems Phase

Build the machine that keeps
customers without paying for them twice.

Phase 2 installs the retention, content, and automation systems that turn the store into a revenue machine that doesn't depend on manual effort or constant new acquisition. It creates compounding value — email flows that run without you, content that produces consistently, reviews that build trust automatically, and workflows that reduce execution overhead.

Phase Objective

Install the lifecycle systems that capture, nurture, and recover revenue that would otherwise be lost — welcome flows, cart recovery, reviews, social content, and the automation layer that orchestrates it all.

Business Problem Solved

Without retention infrastructure, every customer acquired through paid or organic channels has a ceiling — they buy once and disappear. Phase 2 builds the systems that raise that ceiling and create repeat purchase economics.

Phase 2 Outcome

Welcome and cart recovery flows are live and performing. Social content publishes consistently. Reviews are appearing on product pages. Automation workflows are reducing manual work. Email is generating 15%+ of total revenue.

Implementation Deliverables

Email / CRM / Retention Systems

Welcome Flow — 3 to 5 Email Sequence

Brand story → scent education → first purchase incentive. The welcome flow is typically the highest-revenue-per-subscriber email sequence a brand will ever send. This must be built before any meaningful list growth occurs.

Layer 5

Abandoned Cart Recovery Flow

A 2–3 email sequence triggered when a cart is abandoned. Targets the highest-intent segment of all site visitors — people who chose a product and almost bought it. Industry benchmark: 5–15% recovery rate on cold.

Layer 5

Browse Abandonment Flow

Re-engagement sequence for subscribers who viewed products without adding to cart. Softer than cart recovery — focuses on product discovery, scent storytelling, and soft nudges rather than urgency.

Layer 5

Post-Purchase Follow-Up & Review Request

Thank-you email with care instructions, usage tips, and a structured review request trigger. Review generation must be built into the operational system — not done manually one by one.

Layer 5

Newsletter Infrastructure & Broadcast Cadence

Monthly or biweekly newsletter structure with a consistent brand voice. Keeps the list warm between purchase cycles and creates a direct communication channel that doesn't depend on platform algorithms.

Layer 5

Social Proof Infrastructure

Review Collection System Activation

Configure AiTrillion's review request triggers in the post-purchase flow. Set a target of 10+ verified reviews across top products by end of Phase 2. Reviews are social proof that replaces the credibility that ad spend alone cannot buy.

Layer 5

Review Display on Product Pages

Ensure reviews appear visibly on product pages with star ratings visible in collection view. The presence of genuine reviews materially improves cold traffic conversion rate — this is measurable infrastructure, not just aesthetics.

3
Growth Phase

Activate the acquisition engine —
on a foundation built to hold it.

Phase 3 is where Darity graduates from building a brand into scaling one. With a proven store, active retention systems, and a running content engine underneath it, paid acquisition, marketplace expansion, and systematic creative testing can now be deployed against a foundation that will hold the weight. Without Phases 1 and 2 in place, Phase 3 would be premature.

Phase Objective

Activate paid acquisition, Etsy marketplace launch, creative testing infrastructure, and advanced retention — with all the measurement, architecture, and content systems from Phases 1 and 2 already functioning underneath them.

Business Problem Solved

A brand with no paid acquisition, no marketplace presence, and no systematic creative testing has a hard ceiling on revenue. Phase 3 removes that ceiling — intelligently, not aggressively — with a blended efficiency framework keeping it accountable.

Phase 3 Outcome

Paid social is live with a structured testing cadence. Etsy is generating organic sales. Repeat purchase and loyalty flows are running. The business has a measurable, blended acquisition and retention engine with a clear path to Q2 expansion.

Implementation Deliverables

Paid Acquisition Launch

Meta Ads Account Structure & Audience Architecture

Campaign structure built for Phase 3's traffic volume: cold prospecting campaigns separated from warm retargeting campaigns. Audience architecture that distinguishes new visitors from cart abandoners, product viewers, and past purchasers. This separation prevents budget mixing and enables clean optimization decisions.

Layer 2Layer 4

Cold Traffic Initial Creative Set

First paid ad creative batch using Phase 2 AI-produced assets: UGC-style video, lifestyle imagery, and scent storytelling formats. Multiple angles tested simultaneously against the defined Phase 3 KPI thresholds.

Layer 3

Retargeting Campaign Architecture

Dedicated retargeting campaigns for site visitors, add-to-cart segments, and past purchasers. The Meta Pixel installed in Phase 1 has been accumulating audience data throughout Phases 1 and 2 — this is when that data is deployed.

Layer 4

Paid Traffic Launch Protocol & Scaling Rules

A defined set of conditions that must be met before budget is increased: minimum conversion volume, cost-per-purchase threshold, blended ROAS floor, and creative performance benchmarks. Scaling without these rules amplifies waste rather than profitable acquisition.

Layer 4

Creative Testing Engine

Systematic Creative Testing Framework

A disciplined brief-to-test-to-analyze process: creative angle identified → brief written → AI-assisted production → campaign test → performance review → iteration or kill decision. This prevents creative concentration risk — the single greatest threat to paid performance beyond early-stage brands.

Layer 3Layer 4

Creative Angle Diversification — 5+ Angles

Identify and develop at least 5 distinct creative angles for cold traffic testing: sensory/scent storytelling, gifting occasion, lifestyle aspiration, ingredient story, social proof. Angle diversification prevents the single-creative dependency that collapses paid performance when one ad fatigues.

Layer 3

Rolling Creative Backlog & Review Cadence

Maintain a rolling backlog of 10+ creative concepts ready to enter production. Weekly creative performance review cadence: what's working, what's fatiguing, what needs to be replaced. This cadence is more important than any single ad's performance.

Layer 3Layer 4

Measurement & Optimization Infrastructure

Blended Efficiency Tracking Setup

Track Marketing Efficiency Ratio (total revenue ÷ total ad spend) as the primary paid acquisition health metric — not platform-reported ROAS, which overcounts. A spread greater than 40% between platform ROAS and blended MER is a measurement integrity problem, not a performance problem.

Layer 1Layer 4

Full-Funnel Performance Review SOP

A documented weekly and monthly review process covering paid traffic performance, email contribution, organic content reach, and combined revenue trends. Decision frameworks specifying when to scale, hold, or cut based on defined thresholds — not instinct.

Layer 4

Etsy Marketplace Expansion

Etsy Storefront Launch

Launch Etsy storefront using Phase 1 scent-led product copy as the primary listing content base. The copy work done in Phase 1 was the prerequisite — Etsy listings written from scratch in Phase 3 alone would waste time already invested.

Layer 2

Etsy SEO Optimization

Keyword-optimized titles, tags, and listing descriptions tuned for Etsy's internal search algorithm. Etsy is a high-intent marketplace — buyers on Etsy are searching with purchase intent, not browsing. SEO-optimized listings capture that intent before a competitor does.

Layer 1

Etsy Review Generation Strategy

An early review generation plan for the new storefront. Etsy reviews are more visible than Shopify reviews in marketplace search — getting the first 5–10 reviews accelerates organic marketplace ranking significantly.

Layer 5

Etsy Operating Rhythm

Monthly Etsy maintenance cadence: listing review, inventory alignment, pricing check, customer message response, and review monitoring. Etsy rewards consistent engagement — absence from the platform slows algorithmic favor.

Layer 5

Advanced Retention & Loyalty

Repeat-Purchase Campaign Series

Dedicated email sequences designed to re-engage existing buyers based on purchase history and timing. A fragrance buyer who loved their first candle is the highest-probability buyer for a second — but only if the brand reminds them at the right moment.

Layer 5

Winback Flow — 60–90 Day Lapsed Buyers

An automated re-engagement sequence for customers who haven't purchased in 60–90 days. Typically uses scent storytelling + a compelling return incentive. The winback flow is low-cost to operate and creates meaningful repeat purchase revenue without acquiring a single new customer.

Layer 5

Customer Segmentation — High-Value vs One-Time Buyers

Basic segmentation of the customer list into high-value buyers, one-time purchasers, and lapsed customers. Different retention strategies apply to each group. This segmentation makes every campaign more relevant and every automation more efficient.

Layer 5

Q2+ Expansion Preparation

Q2 Strategic Review & Expansion Decision

A formal quarterly review assessing whether the business metrics support expansion into Amazon, scent subscriptions, corporate gifting, or wholesale. Amazon and subscription models should only be entered once the Shopify + Etsy foundation proves it can hold the operational complexity. This review prevents premature expansion that dilutes focus before the core engine is proven.

Layer 4Layer 5

  • Repeat purchase rate trending toward 15%+
  • Email contributing 20%+ of total revenue including retention flows

How to Choose Your Tier

Lean, balanced, or accelerated —
here's how to decide.

Not every tool needs to be active immediately, and not every phase requires the same level of investment. Use this guidance to match your current stage, budget, and ambition to the right tier path — and understand what you're trading off at each level.

◈

Mandatory — Always subscribe

These tools are non-negotiable for executing their respective phase properly. Removing any of them doesn't save money — it breaks the deliverable that depends on them, which typically costs more to recover than the subscription cost saved.

Subscribe when

  • The tool is listed as Mandatory in that phase
  • The phase you are entering begins
  • No viable substitute covers the same function
Optimal Default Path
◈

Essential — The tier where the best work happens

The Balanced path — Mandatory tools plus Essentials — is where maximum value is delivered. It's not a compromise between lean and accelerated. It's the path where every phase is executed to its full potential: the right tools, doing real work, producing compounding results that a Mandatory-only stack simply cannot match.

In Phase 1, adding OpenArt expands the creative range available for brand visuals, and AiTrillion starts building the subscriber list that Phase 2's email flows depend on — every subscriber captured during Phase 1 becomes revenue recovered in Phase 2. In Phase 2, adding Runway ML, Synthetic UGC, and Metricool completes the content engine with polished video outputs, UGC-style variety, and consistent publishing across all three platforms. In Phase 3, the full Balanced stack represents the complete operating system for a brand running paid acquisition, retention automation, and marketplace expansion simultaneously — everything you need, nothing you don't.

  • Phase 1: OpenArt expands visual creative range; AiTrillion list-building pays forward into Phase 2 email revenue
  • Phase 2: complete content engine — polished video (Runway ML), UGC variety (Synthetic UGC), consistent scheduling (Metricool)
  • Phase 3: the full operating stack for paid acquisition + retention + Etsy at $408/month cumulative
  • Every Essential adds a measurable capability that cannot be replicated with manual effort at scale

The right choice when

  • You want the work done at its highest standard, not merely its minimum viable version
  • Each phase's full capability — not just its core function — matters to the outcome
  • Revenue from the Mandatory layer is active and the Essential additions are the next logical step
◻

Enhancers — Defer until earned

Enhancers are force multipliers — they increase speed, polish, leverage, and creative throughput. But they only multiply something that's already working. Adding Enhancers before the Mandatory and Essential layers are operating effectively is investing in acceleration before direction is established.

Add when

  • The Mandatory stack for that phase is fully operational
  • A specific capability gap the Enhancer fills has become a visible bottleneck
  • Paid acquisition is active and creative quality or landing page flexibility is measurably limiting performance

Strategic Summary

Three phases. One coherent
architecture for growth.

This roadmap is not a collection of tactics. It is a sequenced architecture — each phase creates the conditions the next phase depends on. The result is a brand that earns traffic intelligently, retains customers systematically, and scales acquisition only when the foundation is proven strong enough to support it.

1

A store worth sending traffic to

Product copy that earns the purchase. Trust infrastructure that removes hesitation. Tracking that makes every decision accountable. Offer architecture that improves revenue without requiring more visitors. This is the floor everything else is built on.

2

Systems that compound without extra effort

Welcome flows, cart recovery, content engines, review infrastructure, lead magnets, and automation workflows that run reliably once built. These systems improve LTV, reduce acquisition dependency, and create margin — the economic foundation that makes Phase 3 worth investing in.

3

A scalable acquisition engine on solid ground

Paid social, creative testing, Etsy expansion, and blended efficiency measurement — deployed against a store that converts, a retention system that recaptures customers, and a tracking layer that tells the truth. This is how scaling becomes profitable rather than expensive.

The Principle Behind the Plan

Every phase is designed around a single conviction: the infrastructure that allows a brand to scale profitably must be built before the acquisition spend that demands it. Not the other way around.

The brands that scale predictably are not the ones that spend the most. They are the ones that built the clearest foundation, the strongest retention mechanics, and the most systematic creative testing process before they ever opened the paid traffic tap.
